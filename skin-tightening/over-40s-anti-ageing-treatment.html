FAQs — HIFU for Over-40s Anti-Ageing Treatment

Q: Which of the skin changes occurring between 40 and 50 years can HIFU address?

A: After age 40, production of natural collagen and elastin in the skin decelerates even more. Typical changes include more defined lines, less firmness (particularly around your jawline, cheeks and neck), droopy skin, thinner texture and slower healing. “HIFU achieves this effect by inducing collagen production and tightening underlying tissues, which can gradually restore lifted, firmer and more elastic skin.”

Q: Do people in their 40s have good results for HIFU as compared to younger clients?

A: Yes. In actual fact, many people find HIFU really helps them in their 40’s since this is when we begin to see very visible signs of ageing and thus the changes are more noticeable. Whilst rosy, younger skin has more bounce back and needs less input, with over-40s there’s generally a tauter face (albeit needing some tightening up), better jawline/neck contouring and fewer fine lines. The results may take a bit longer to show and sometimes require more energy or slightly different device settings, but the improvement is often dramatic.

Q: What will HIFU do for me, especially if I’m over the age of 40?

A: Some of the most important are:

  • Enhanced skin laxity & tightness (jaw, neck, cheeks)
  • Diminished look of deeper wrinkles & fine lines
  • Elevated, rejuvenated appearance; particularly under the chin or jowl line
  • Better skin texture and tone
  • Little or no downtime compared to traditional facelift surgeries and other, more in-depth procedures
  • Causual look (doesn't make you Immediately “pulled”).

Q: How fast will I notice results and how long will results last in my 40s?

A: Yes, some initial benefits (such as tightening or lifting) may be seen within the first day, and those tend to consolidate over seven days.

The most obvious changes are usually visible after 8-12 weeks as new collagen forms.

It is six months longer in which total effects might keep improving.

That being said longevity is usually from 12-24 months depending on skin, lifestyle (sun protection would be ideal and smoking can also fade the ink), how well you maintain your skin and if you continue doing touch up or maintenance sessions.

Q: What are the discomfort, side effects and risks of trying microneedling, particularly for older skin?

A: For those over age 40, side effects are not significantly different from those experienced by younger individuals, although skin might be thinner with slower wound healing and be a bit more sensitive. Possible effects include:

  • Some redness, swelling is normal after treatment along with some tingling and warmth.
  • Light bruising or tender sensation in some areas.
  • Transient numbing of small or slight tingling.
  • Rare: additional hyper-pigmentation (if skin exposed to sun after treatment) or very slight risk of nerve injury with inappropriate settings on device.

Q: How should someone above 40 prepare for a HIFU session and what are the things to take care of afterwards?

A: Preparation & after-care work well for more mature skin. Helpful steps include:

  • Do not over- expose the skin to sun. Use SPF daily.
  • Cease strong exfoliants, retinoids or active acids a few days before (you may have been told not to by practitioner).
  • Keep skin well hydrated.
  • Let your provider know about your history, medications and previous cosmetic treatments.
  • Post-treatment: light cleanse, apply moisturiser and sunscreen for 24-48 hours; avoid hot baths/saunas or sweating.
  • Don't have invasive treatments (peels, laser) in the same area for a few weeks afterward.

Q: For whom is not a good candidate or when can HIFU fail to have effect on those over 40?

A: Conditions in which HIFU may be less effective or more dangerous are:

  • Severely droopy, loose, or excessive skin (heavy sagging with overhangs) that may only be addressed by a surgical lift.
  • In cases of extreme volume loss (in the cheeks or mid-face), fillers may be the better option initially.
  • Those with skin conditions such as active acne, open wounds or certain skin conditions in the treatment area.
  • You don’t heal well (due to certain diseases or medicines).
  • Very high expectations: HIFU enhances and doesn’t replace a surgical facelift.

Q: How frequently, if at all, will I need to get maintenance treatments in my 40s?

A: Generally, one course of HIFU produces visible results; to improve and extend these effects, many practitioners recommend follow-ups or “top-ups” every 12-18 months for those in their 40s. How often you choose to go is up to you and your lifestyle (sun protection, good skin care) and how well your skin tones in terms of treatment.
